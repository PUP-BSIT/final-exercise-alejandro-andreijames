My Journal

Reflecting on my journey in the Web Development course, I can't help but see it as a rollercoaster ride, filled with its fair share of lows and highs. At the outset, every zero on an exercise felt like a devastating blow. It was more than just a mark; it felt like a reflection of my inability to keep up with the rapid pace of learning new coding languages each week, such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Git, PHP, HTTP Cookies, and database management. This was particularly challenging as a student with no background in coding from my senior high school days, making it hard to catch up with classmates who had taken ICT courses. The constant cycle of being introduced to new concepts, trying to apply them under the pressure of time, and often failing to complete exercises was incredibly draining. I won't sugarcoat it – it was frustrating and disheartening. There were moments when I questioned not just my competence in the subject but my decision to take this course. However, as time passed, I began to see these challenges in a different light. I realized that these difficulties were not just academic hurdles; they were life lessons. The course was teaching us, in its unique way, what it feels like to be in the field of web development. It was preparing us for the real world, where setbacks are part of the journey and resilience is key. One profound lesson was the emphasis on "Cooperation and Teamwork over Competition." We learned that to achieve something significant, hard work and teamwork are essential. This course was not just about coding; it was about growing as a valuable individual in the IT industry, understanding the importance of proper netiquettes, and how to conduct ourselves professionally in a digital environment. The devastation of those zeroes eventually gave way to learning and understanding. It wasn't just about learning to code; it was about learning to pick myself up after a setback, to persevere through difficulties, and to find solutions in the face of challenges. This realization marked a turning point in my journey. Yes, the course was tiring – perhaps the most exhausting I have ever taken. But it was also the most enriching. Despite the initial frustrations and feelings of being overwhelmed, I have learned immensely. Not just about web development, but about myself, about persistence, and about overcoming obstacles. As I near the end of this course, I look back with a sense of accomplishment. The lows were undoubtedly tough, but they were necessary for the highs – the moments of understanding, the successful completion of a challenging exercise, the sense of achievement after grasping a difficult concept. This course, with all its rigors, has been a profound learning experience, one that I will carry with me as I move forward in my career and life.
